The Mathematical Bridge was designed by William Etheridge, and built by James Essex in 1749. It has been rebuilt on two occasions, in 1866 and in 1905, but has kept the same overall design. Although it appears to be an arch, it is composed entirely of straight timbers built to an unusually sophisticated engineering design, hence the name.

One of the most famous of Cambridge pubs, there is a ceiling with signatures of Second World War pilots. Perhaps more importantly this is where Crick and Watson announced the discovery of the structure of DNA.

The Corpus Clock, also known as the Grasshopper clock, is a large sculptural clock at street level. It was conceived and funded by John C. Taylor, an old member of the college. It was officially unveiled to the public on 19 September 2008 by Cambridge physicist Stephen Hawking.
